### ⚔️ Differentiating a Fakeout vs a Valid Breakout

Follow-through is the ultimate filter.
It’s what separates a clean breakout from a trap designed to milk liquidity.

#### 🧩 1. Structure Confirmation

A strong follow-through candle should:

Open near the breakout level (just above the broken zone or previous high).

Close near its high → shows sustained buying pressure.

Have a small upper wick (<25% of total range ideally).

This shows real commitment from buyers and no meaningful absorption by sellers.

#### 📊 2. Volume Confirmation

Volume tells you who’s behind the move.

Ideally, volume should increase from breakout → follow-through candle.

A good rule: Follow-through volume ≥ 70% of breakout volume.

If volume fades right after breakout, it’s usually a trap or early exhaustion.

Consistent or rising volume = participation by institutions, not retailers chasing green candles.

### 🧭 3. Location & Context

A valid follow-through should appear:

Above key reference levels (breakout zone, VWAP, recent resistance).

Never into resistance — that’s usually a rug pull disguised as continuation.

Always ask: “Is price breaking away from trouble or into trouble?”

#### 🚫 4. Signs of a Weak Follow-Through

Avoid trades where:

The candle closes far from its high (large upper wick).

Volume drops versus the breakout candle.

Price stalls or instantly retests the breakout zone.

Those setups are often fake continuations — engineered stop-hunts before reversal.
