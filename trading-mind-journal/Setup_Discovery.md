Kid, if you ever decide to trade, remember this: don’t expect someone else to hand you a profitable setup. In trading,
real edges are usually discovered, not given.

When I first started, I asked ChatGPT what long setups I should use. It suggested a few popular ones, and I picked two —
ORB and VWAP reclaim. Many traders used them, so I assumed they must work.

I began trading them live. Some trades worked, some didn’t, but the results were inconsistent. Over time I realized
something important: when a setup becomes popular, everyone is watching the same levels — including algorithms and big
players — and the market often traps the crowd.

One day I decided to do something different. Instead of searching for setups online, I started reviewing the charts of
the top gaining stocks every day. I simply studied them and asked myself one question: *what actually happened here?*

While doing that, I noticed something interesting. Many of those stocks had a strong surge in volume right after the
market opened, and then they started moving upward.

No one taught me that setup. I noticed it myself.

So I went back and studied hundreds of charts. I carefully backtested the idea to see if the pattern repeated often
enough to matter. And the results were promising.

That experience taught me an important lesson about trading: if you want a real edge, study the best performers, observe
carefully, recognize patterns, and then test your ideas before risking money.

And remember — this same process can be applied to almost anything you want to learn in life.
